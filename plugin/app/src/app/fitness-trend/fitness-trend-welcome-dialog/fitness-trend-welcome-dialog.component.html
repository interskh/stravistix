New feature important notice: "Estimated Fitness Trend"

From now, if you don't own (or sometimes forgot) your sensors, your fitness trend can be nevertheless estimated. Extensively a "typical" iPhone or Android mobile user who runs the strava mobile app can now access the fitness trend feature without buying any external sensors üëç. Nevertheless sensors are highly recommended if you need a more accurate trend.
Since the fitness trend is calculated on activities' stress scores, this implies that stress scores of your activities without sensors have to be estimated too. Currently only cycling, running and swimming activities can have their stress scores estimated.
Estimating stress scores on cycling and running implies the below prerequisites:
  • Estimated Cycling Power Stress Score (PSS):
    • Your Cycling Functional Threshold Power (FTP) must be set in athlete settings.
    • The power meter data toggle flash_on must be switched ON (on screen behind).
    • Your weight must be set in strava profile. (If not set strava can't estimate the power data on your cycling activities required to compute PSS.
  • Estimated Running Stress Score (RSS):
    • Your Running Functional Threshold Pace must be set in athlete settings.
warning The "Estimated Fitness Trend" may not work properly on existing synced activities because some added stats (required to estimate stress scores and fitness trend) are missing on your currently synced activities. If you need to estimate stress scores on some of your activities and you have already synced your activities in the past, you may do this:
  • Perform a backup of your synced activities for security before. Just click sync icon then file_download Backup activities
  • Make sure you properly configured all your athlete settings.
  • Clear and synchronize again your activities. Click sync icon then redo Clear and re-sync activities
